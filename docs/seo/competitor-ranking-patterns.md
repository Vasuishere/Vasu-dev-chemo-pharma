# Competitor Ranking Patterns

Based on the competitor URL inventory in `merged_9204df5ce798aa2c.csv` and the target terms we are pursuing, the pages that rank most consistently follow the same pattern:

1. Exact-match product intent
Pages use the search phrase directly in the slug, title, H1, and opening copy. Examples include `monoethanolamine-triazine`, `h2s-scavenger`, `water-treatment`, and branded terms like `jadescan-54` or `nipacide-bk`.

2. Brand-plus-alternative intent
Many searches are brand-led, not generic. Competitor brands such as JadeScan 54, Tacelene Chem, HSCAV-70, Kwantum, and Nipacide BK rank because buyers search the brand first, then look for an equivalent or manufacturer.

3. Application-specific relevance
Water treatment, metalworking fluids, and drilling fluids rank because those pages are not trying to rank only for `MEA Triazine`. They match the end use directly with terms like `water treatment biocide`, `metalworking fluid preservative`, and `drilling fluids`.

4. India commercial modifiers
Searches such as `manufacturer in India`, `supplier India`, `Gujarat manufacturer`, and `bulk supplier` are strong because they signal direct buying intent. Indian competitor pages win by combining product chemistry with location-specific commercial modifiers.

5. Technical chemistry signals
CAS number references, chemical identity terms, concentration grades like `70%` and `78%`, and phrases such as `monoethanolamine triazine` help pages rank for technical buyers comparing equivalence, qualification, and dosing fit.

6. Supporting document intent
Queries with `TDS`, `MSDS`, `SDS`, `technical data sheet`, and `specification` often appear alongside product searches. These terms are common on ranking competitor pages because they align with how procurement teams search.

## What this means for Vasudev

- We should keep building around exact-match brand comparison pages.
- We should keep building application pages for end-use searches, not only product-name searches.
- We should use India commercial modifiers heavily on the product page and India supply page.
- We should keep technical chemistry terms and CAS references visible on commercial pages.
- We should support every major brand keyword with an equivalent, alternative, or comparison route.
